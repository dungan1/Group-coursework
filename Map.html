Taihe Palace
Taihe Palace, commonly known as the "Jinluan Palace", is located in a prominent position on the north-south main axis of the Forbidden City, where the emperor accepts the congratulations of civil and military officials here, and gives banquets to the princes and ministers.
Yongshou Palace
Yongshou Palace is one of the six palaces in the west of the inner court, which is the residence of Zhen Huan after returning to the palace, and it is here that she started the dragon slaying plan.
Gyeongin Palace
Gyeongin Palace, one of the six eastern palaces of the Inner Court, is the residence of the queen and the base of the queen's power.
Yikun Palace
Yikun Palace, one of the six palaces in the west of the inner court, is the residence of Concubine Hua, and it is also the base of Concubine Hua's power.
Yanxi Palace
Yanxi Palace is one of the six palaces in the east of the inner court, and it is An Lingrong's residence, witnessing An Lingrong's transformation from Zhen Huan's friend to enemy.
Xianfu Palace
Xianfu Palace is one of the six palaces in the west of the inner court, the residence of Shen Guiren and Concubine Jing, and an important strategic base for Zhen Huan.
Shoukang Palace
Shoukang Palace, located on the West Road outside the inner court, is the sole residence of the Queen Mother, providing an excellent geographical advantage for the Queen Mother to secretly explore the undercurrent of the harem from the outside.
Yangxin Palace
Yangxin Palace, located on the west side of the Qianqing Palace in the inner court, is a place for the emperor to offer Buddha and rest.
Imperial Garden
The Imperial Garden is located on the central axis of the Forbidden City, behind the Kunning Palace, and is built for the emperor and concubine to rest and enjoy.
